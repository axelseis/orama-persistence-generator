# Glassmorphism

## Definition
A modern UI style based on translucent layers, background blur, and vibrant gradients. It creates depth and hierarchy while keeping a premium, lightweight feel.

## Intent and use
- Communicate modernity, elegance, and digital sophistication.
- Ideal for dashboards, fintech, creative tools, and premium SaaS.

## Color palettes (examples)
- **Frosted Blue**: #EAF6FF (glass), #C7E0FF (light), #8FB9FF (accent), #2C3A4A (text), #0F141A (dark)
- **Icy Violet**: #F3ECFF (glass), #D8C6FF (soft), #9B7BFF (accent), #2A2A2A (text), #121212 (dark)
- **Aurora**: #F7F8FB (glass), #A8E6FF (cyan), #C6A0FF (violet), #FFB6C7 (pink), #1C1C1C (text)

## Design rules
- **Layers**: use translucent panels with background blur.
- **Borders**: soft, semi-transparent strokes.
- **Depth**: subtle shadows to separate glass layers.
- **Backgrounds**: gradients or abstract color fields.
- **Motion**: slow, smooth transitions and fade-ins.

## Typography (fonts available in Penpot)
Rounded modern sans that read well over translucency.
- **Headings**: "Plus Jakarta Sans", "Manrope", "Urbanist", "Sora"
- **Body**: same family in regular/medium
- **UI**: same family in medium/semibold

## Layout and components
- **Cards** with blur and soft drop shadow.
- **Modals** with strong blur and subtle border.
- **Buttons** with glass fill and bright accent hover.

## Illustration and imagery
- Soft gradients, glow effects, and floating shapes.
- Minimal icons with light strokes.

## Accessibility
- Maintain high contrast between text and glass panels.
- Avoid text directly over busy backgrounds.

## Agent keywords
glass, translucent, blur, frosted, gradient, depth, premium, modern
