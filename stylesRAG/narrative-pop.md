# Narrative Pop

## Definition
An editorial, story-driven style that uses dense layouts, layered typography, and rich hierarchy. It turns design surfaces into readable, immersive narratives.

## Intent and use
- Encourage exploration and long-form reading.
- Ideal for editorials, storytelling landing pages, campaigns, and content-heavy brands.

## Color palettes (examples)
- **Editorial Ink**: #F7F2E9 (paper), #1F1F1F (text), #B05A3C (rust), #6C7A8C (blue-gray), #D8C9B3 (tan)
- **Modern Magazine**: #FFFFFF (white), #2B2B2B (charcoal), #E4572E (accent red), #1D4E89 (blue), #F0EDE8 (warm gray)
- **Warm Spread**: #F5EEE3 (paper), #3A3A3A (text), #C27D5B (clay), #A8B3A1 (sage), #E0D4C2 (kraft)

## Design rules
- **Hierarchy**: large headlines, strong subheads, readable body text.
- **Density**: intentional content density with clear spacing zones.
- **Layers**: overlapping text blocks and image captions.
- **Rhythm**: vary scale and weight to guide reading flow.

## Typography (fonts available in Penpot)
Pair expressive display with strong text faces.
- **Headlines**: "Playfair Display", "DM Serif Display", "Cinzel", "Literata"
- **Subheads / UI**: "Work Sans", "Inter", "IBM Plex Sans", "Public Sans"
- **Body**: "Merriweather", "Libre Baskerville", "Spectral", "Crimson Text", "Newsreader"

## Layout and components
- **Hero** with oversized headline and supporting deck.
- **Pull quotes** and sidebars for emphasis.
- **Image captions** and callouts as part of the narrative.

## Illustration and imagery
- Editorial photography, strong cropping, and captions.
- Collage elements and archival imagery for context.

## Accessibility
- Keep line length comfortable and line-height generous.
- Maintain strong contrast for long reading sessions.
