# Imprinted

## Definition
A tactile print-driven style that highlights manual marks: embossing, ink texture, and visible print artifacts. It conveys craftsmanship and a human-made process.

## Intent and use
- Emphasize authenticity, sustainability, and artisanal value.
- Ideal for handmade goods, boutique packaging, and local brands.

## Color palettes (examples)
- **Pressed Paper**: #F4EFE5 (paper), #D2C2AD (kraft), #8A6F5A (brown), #2F2F2F (ink), #C76B4B (rust)
- **Ink Stamp**: #F7F4EE (off-white), #1E1E1E (black), #B45A3C (rust), #6E7A6D (sage), #C0B39E (tan)
- **Workshop**: #F2E8D8 (cream), #B98E6B (sepia), #7C5A48 (wood), #3A3A3A (charcoal), #A96A52 (clay)

## Design rules
- **Texture**: show ink bleed, paper grain, or stamped edges.
- **Alignment**: slight misalignment is acceptable and human.
- **Type**: mix expressive accents with clean body text.
- **Ornament**: minimal but tactile, like stamps or seals.

## Typography (fonts available in Penpot)
Expressive accents with readable body type.
- **Accents / short titles**: "Indie Flower", "Architects Daughter", "Caveat", "Rock Salt", "Patrick Hand"
- **Body / long text**: "Public Sans", "Commissioner", "Inter", "IBM Plex Sans"
- **Optional serif**: "Spectral", "Merriweather"

## Layout and components
- **Labels** with stamped corners or embossed borders.
- **Badges** as ink stamps or hand-pressed seals.
- **Dividers** as rough lines or brush strokes.

## Illustration and imagery
- Scanned paper textures, ink blots, and stamped marks.
- Hand-inked doodles or simple analog icons.

## Accessibility
- Avoid heavy texture under small text.
- Maintain strong contrast for labels and ingredients.
