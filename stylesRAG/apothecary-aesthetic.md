# Apothecary Aesthetic

## Definition
A botanical vintage style inspired by old scientific labels and apothecary packaging. It uses rigid grids, classic serifs, and archival illustration to convey trust, care, and tradition.

## Intent and use
- Communicate natural ingredients, expertise, and heritage.
- Ideal for wellness, supplements, cosmetics, and specialty beverages.
- Balances clinical clarity with handcrafted charm.

## Color palettes (examples)
- **Botanical Archive**: #F4EFE5 (paper), #2B2B2B (ink), #7B8C6E (sage), #B08B5A (sepia), #8E4B3A (rust)
- **Herbal Lab**: #F7F2E7 (cream), #3E4A40 (forest), #9AA48A (olive), #C6B39B (parchment), #1D1F1E (text)
- **Vintage Tonic**: #F1E9DC (paper), #4A4E45 (charcoal), #8A6F5A (brown), #BFAE94 (tan), #6F7B6A (moss)

## Design rules
- **Grid**: structured, symmetrical layouts and centered labels.
- **Ornaments**: subtle borders, frames, and line dividers.
- **Illustration**: botanical drawings, scientific diagrams, herb line art.
- **Texture**: paper grain and ink impression, kept subtle.

## Typography (fonts available in Penpot)
Classic serifs for authority, clean supporting text for readability.
- **Headings / labels**: "Cinzel", "EB Garamond", "Old Standard TT", "Goudy Bookletter 1911"
- **Subheads / technical**: "Spectral", "Lora", "Crimson Text"
- **Body**: "Libre Baskerville", "Merriweather", "Newsreader"

## Layout and components
- **Labels** with framed borders and centered typography.
- **Tables** for ingredient facts or dosage.
- **Badges** as seals or medallions.
- **Icons** in thin line, botanical or scientific style.

## Illustration and imagery
- Hand-drawn herb sketches, anatomical plants, vintage diagrams.
- Subtle aging or paper tinting.

## Accessibility
- Keep body text size comfortable and high contrast.
- Avoid overly ornate type in long text.
