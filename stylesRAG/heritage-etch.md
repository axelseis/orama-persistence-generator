# Heritage Etch

## Definition
A premium heritage style based on engraving, hatching, and fine-line illustration. It signals tradition, authority, and meticulous craftsmanship.

## Intent and use
- Emphasize legacy, provenance, and premium quality.
- Ideal for spirits, luxury goods, boutique services, and classic brands.

## Color palettes (examples)
- **Etched Ink**: #F6F1E7 (paper), #2A2A2A (ink), #6B5B4D (sepia), #A18C72 (tan), #3B3B3B (text)
- **Copperplate**: #F2E9DC (parchment), #3E3A36 (charcoal), #8F6B4F (copper), #C7B59A (tan), #1F1F1F (text)
- **Old Ledger**: #F5EFE4 (paper), #4B4B4B (graphite), #8A7C6B (brown), #BFAE96 (parchment), #202020 (text)

## Design rules
- **Linework**: thin strokes, cross-hatching, stippling.
- **Composition**: centered, balanced, and formal.
- **Texture**: subtle paper grain; avoid loud noise.
- **Spacing**: generous margins and clear type hierarchy.

## Typography (fonts available in Penpot)
Classic serif families with refined details.
- **Headings**: "Cinzel", "EB Garamond", "Old Standard TT", "IM Fell Double Pica"
- **Body**: "Libre Baskerville", "Merriweather", "Spectral", "Newsreader"
- **Small caps / accents**: "Cinzel", "Cormorant SC"

## Layout and components
- **Frames** with thin lines and corner ornaments.
- **Seals** with engraved style icons.
- **Tables** and specs with lined separators.

## Illustration and imagery
- Engraved animals, crests, coats of arms.
- Vintage product illustrations in monochrome.

## Accessibility
- Keep fine lines away from small text.
- Ensure strong contrast for readability.
